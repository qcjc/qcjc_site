#1 - 10/03/2024

From the perspective of algorithmic designers, it is a marvelous revolution to discover how algorithms run on real machines. There are whole worlds behind the implementation of a quantum computer. Each hardware has its advantages and disadvantages. The interesting question that quantum computing brought to the surface is how we link the algorithmic procedure with the physical implementation of our tool. Studying quantum computing means diving into the possibilities of a physical system to do something that has never been made before. In constructing algorithms, we usually use gates as they are achievable, but there is a whole world of difficulties behind them. Here we highlight some methods and algorithms, trying to target some hardware solutions. We do not aim to be complete about a topic, but we found these interesting papers as a starting point!

  • 1) Long-lived valley states in bilayer graphene quantum dots (possibilities and limitations of quantum dots)
  • 2) Nonlocal Finite-Depth Circuits for Constructing Symmetry-Protected Topological States and Quantum Cellular Automata (related to topological quantum computing)
  • 3) Non-Abelian Anyons and Topological Quantum Computation (this is at the basis of Quantinuum quantum computer using ion trap)
  • 4) Two-dimensional momentum state lattices (how to use laser-atom interaction)
  • 5) Distributed quantum sensing of multiple phases with fewer photons (metrology's importance for quantum communications)
